Congratulations {{name}},

You have been awarded a new client! At RealtyOffer, we like to think long-term. This is not about you winning one bid or making thousands of dollars – this is about building your business and winning a lifelong client!

  1. You will notice a downloadable PDF in the upper right corner of your awarded offer page. This PDF entails the terms and conditions you have offered and agreed to. Your new client will have the same terms and conditions to reference to.

  2. Prepare to shine, impress your client and contact them immediately. Highlight your credentials and make them comfortable. Congratulate the client and provide them with a detailed plan of action for their listing and purchase. Your client will be expecting this!

  3. Upcoming Listing:

    Your awarded client will expect a listing contract that will entail a reduced commission structure, which will match your downloadable PDF terms and conditions. A standard listing contract is typically signed for a term of 3 months. This time frame can vary based on your location and season during which you list a home.

    Now that you are listing your client’s home, you will need to protect your client and think ahead. Do you think the home needs any repairs or improvements? Prepare your client’s home by requesting a certified home inspector through RealtyCert.

    RealtyOffer recommends a pre-inspection before your listing goes live.

    They will prepare a 125-point home PRE-inspection; this will allow you to complete any repairs or improvements before your home goes on the market. Our affiliates are standing by and are ready to help you through the process.

  4. Upcoming purchase:

    In order for an agent to offer part of their commission, they will need to create an addendum to the purchase agreement. This addendum will show the same commission amount that was agreed upon. Once executed, you will need to submit it to your client’s mortgage company (if any) and title company of choice. The title company will simply deduct the amount from the agent’s commission. Your mortgage company will simply apply the commission towards the closing costs and pre-paid items (just like seller’s concession) – this means less money your client brings to the closing table.

  5. You may want to present an exclusive agency agreement. This is a standard legal document that some real estate brokerages require their clients and agents to sign. Inform your client that if, for any reason, they no longer wish to use your service, they may terminate this agreement via email.

  6. Now that you have engaged with your client, RealtyOffer is here to help you think ahead. Do you need an experienced home lending advisor to pre-approve your client? Does your client need a second review? Don't miss out on the home of your client’s dreams by not being prepared. Partner up with one of our home lending advising affiliates!
